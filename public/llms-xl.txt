## Legal Context

### Missouri Tax Sales (RSMo Chapter 140)
- Property tax due December 31
- 1% per month penalty on unpaid balance
- Tax lien certificate sold at public auction (August)
- Jackson County: 2nd Monday (Independence) & 3rd Monday (Kansas City)
- Clay & Platte Counties: 4th Monday of August
- Owner retains property after lien sale
- 1-year redemption period: full lien + 10% interest + costs
- If not redeemed: collector's deed → ownership transfers permanently
- Reassessment cycle: every 2 years (odd years)

### Missouri Probate (RSMo Chapter 473)
- Independent administration: sell property with minimal court oversight
- Supervised administration: requires court approval for each transaction
- Letters Testamentary: court document authorizing estate representative to sell
- Small-estate affidavit: available if personal property <= $40,000
- Electronic wills recognized after August 28, 2025
- Typical timeline: 6-18 months
- Property can be sold during probate with appropriate authorization

### Kansas Tax Foreclosure (K.S.A. 79-2801)
- Property tax due December 20
- County attorney files judicial foreclosure in district court
- Sheriff's sale ordered by the court
- Redemption right expires day BEFORE sheriff's sale
- NO post-sale redemption in Kansas
- Sheriff's deed records → ownership transfers permanently
- Reassessment: annually

### Kansas Probate (K.S.A. Chapter 59)
- Unsupervised administration (most common): broad authority
- Supervised administration: requires court approval
- Transfer-on-death (TOD) deeds: bypass probate entirely
- Summary administration: available if estate <= $40,000
- Typical timeline: 6-12 months
